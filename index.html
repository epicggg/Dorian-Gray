The Portrait of Dorian Gray

Influence and Corruption

Hedonism

Lord Henry introduces Dorian to a life of pleasure and beauty, promoting a hedonistic lifestyle.

"And Dorian was influenced by Lord Henry more and more. When Dorian was alone, he was always looking for pleasurable things to do."

Ignored Consequences

Henry encourages Dorian to seek new experiences without considering the consequences.

"I choose to live for pleasure and not regret the past."

Lost Morality

This influence leads Dorian to lose his morals and become corrupt.

"You have changed, Dorian. You used to be so kind and innocent."

Impact on Others

Dorian's actions tragically affect the lives of others, like Basil and Sybil Vane.

"Harry was influenced by you."
